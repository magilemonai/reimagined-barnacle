World name:
Valisar / The Eldspyre
1. World Overview
Valisar: Valisar is an epic high fantasy realm inspired by the aesthetics and adventures of Zelda games and Miyazaki films like Spirited Away, Howl’s Moving Castle, and Princess Mononoke. A powerful cosmic artifact called The Eldspyre is the source of all magic in Valisar, lost to time and only referenced in archaeological carvings from an ancient civilization. Modern society refers to the source of magic as "The Spark," or “The Forge” or “The Seed”, which also serves as the connecting point to the Spirit Realm—a portal between the Material Realm and the Spirit World. A key theme of this campaign is the misinterpretation or misunderstanding of The Eldspyre by various cultures and classes. The PCs will uncover its nature, mysteries, and true name as the campaign progresses.

The Citidaea
The City of Liquid Stone
Located in the burning west of the Fuhran Desert, The Citidaea is not merely a city; it is a monument to the arrogance of intellect. Formerly the Kingdom of Aeldrya, it was once a land of enchanted forests and ornate beauty before it fell to the Empire.
Now, it is a labyrinthine metropolis inspired by organic, melting architecture. Towers spiral skyward in fluid, curvilinear forms that defy physics, appearing as if the stone itself is liquid, flowing and melding into a complex tapestry .
The Citidaea functions less like a university and more like a massive defense contractor. The "Seals" are not just schools of thought; they are Directorates in a unified Military-Industrial complex feeding the Sakazarac War Machine.
Here is the organizational breakdown of The Imperial Arcane Directorate, outlining their current classified projects and how the Department Heads will receive your "provincial" party.
The Hierarchy of Seals
The Citidaea was originally governed by Eight Seals, representing the eight schools of magic, led by an Imperator. However, when King Tyserion (The Golden Blade) conquered the region, he forced the creation of a Ninth Seal.
* The Ninth Seal: This Archmage reports directly to the King of Aurengate (Izuriel), bypassing the Imperator entirely. It is the Empire’s leash on the city.
* Imperator Amegmon Shazz: The current leader of the Citidaea. He chafes under Izuriel’s thumb and plots treason, but he is blinded by his own hubris.
* Archmage Severyn Caldreth: The leader of the Ninth Seal and Imperial Liaison. A former soldier with a magical scar and a mechanical eye, he plays Shazz for a fool, lulling him into a false sense of security.

The Dark Influence: The Spirit of Pride
The Entity: The Spirit of Pride manifests within the deep sublevels of the Citidaea as a colossal spider covered in unblinking eyes.
* This spirit intoxicates Imperator Shazz with ego, preventing him from being cunning enough to overthrow the Empire.
* Historically, this spirit was responsible for distributing "dragon madness" to the Wizards of the War of the Fallen.


How It Works (The Silo Effect): The Spirit does not use "mind control" in a crude sense. Instead, it weaponizes The Dunning-Kruger Effect on a god-like scale.
1. Siloed Genius: It amplifies the wizards' belief that their specific field (e.g., Evocation) is the only one that matters, and that other schools are inferior. This prevents the Seals from ever uniting against the Emperor.
2. The Illusion of Control: Every Archmage believes they are the ones manipulating the Emperor. They think, "Izuriel is just a politician; I am a god of physics. I will humor him until my work is complete." They never realize they are hamsters in a wheel built by the Ninth Seal.
3. Intoxication: The Spirit emits a psychic hum that makes being correct feel like a drug. Admitting fault or asking for help causes physical pain. This is why Imperator Amegmon Shazz cannot effectively plot treason—he is too busy gloating about plans he hasn't even executed yet.

Ancient History
Original Comprehensive Timeline Integrating Conflict and Cataclysm
Era of Beginnings (~10,000 - 8,000 years ago)
* 10,000 YA: Awakening of the Eldspyre - The discovery or creation of the Eldspyre ushers in an era of unprecedented unity and power among the ancient sorcerer-kings, who wield it to bring about prosperity and magical advancements.
Era of Expansion (8,000 - 6,000 years ago)
* 7,500 YA: Continental Unity and Harmony - Under the guidance of the Eldspyre, a golden age prevails with sorcerer-kings at the zenith of their power, overseeing a continent unified in peace and magical prowess.
Era of the Great Sundering (6,000 - 5,000 years ago)
* 6,000 YA: Cataclysm and Fragmentation - The misuse of the Eldspyre, possibly due to internal conflicts among the sorcerer-kings or external temptations, leads to a catastrophic event that shatters the continent:
   * Birth of Akuzo and the Ascendant Shadow: In the chaos, negative energies and the darker aspects of the Eldspyre coalesce into malevolent entities like Akuzo and the nascent forms of the Ascendant Shadow. These beings symbolize and perpetuate the cycle of violence and decay.
   * Physical and Magical Schism: The continent fragments into several landmasses, each carrying a part of the Eldspyre's legacy, now distorted or forgotten. This schism ensures ongoing conflict as isolated cultures develop divergent interpretations of their magical heritage.
Era of Dark Ages (5,000 - 3,000 years ago)
* 4,500 YA: Rise of New Empires - As the memory of the Eldspyre fades, new empires rise on the scattered continents, each built on the remnants of ancient power and often coming into conflict over misunderstood or coveted magical legacies.
* 3,500 YA: First Manifestations of the Ascendant Shadow - As corrupt powers grow, the Ascendant Shadow begins to exert its influence more directly, initiating cycles of warfare and corruption.
Era of Rebirth and Enlightenment (3,000 - 1,000 years ago)
* 2,500 YA: Rediscovery and Misinterpretation - Fragments of the original Eldspyre teachings are rediscovered, leading to a flawed renaissance that misinterprets these powers as separate entities like "The Forge" or "The Spark."
* 1,500 YA: The Bone Moon Prophecy - An ominous celestial event linked to the cycles of conflict and the Ascendant Shadow, prophesied to recur and bring great calamity.
Era of the Sakazarac Empire (1,000 years ago - Present)
* 1,000 YA: Founding of the Sakazarac Dynasty - Claiming descent from the ancient sorcerer-kings, the dynasty seeks to unify the fragmented knowledge and restore the glory of the past, unknowingly paving the way for the resurgence of ancient threats.
* Present: The players enter during a tense period of apparent peace shadowed by rising tensions and the looming prophecy of the Bone Moon, with the true history of the Eldspyre lost to time, awaiting rediscovery.
* The Sakazarac empire is run by Izuriel Sakazarac, a young high elf (secretly blood elf) who bargains for great power by becoming a warlock to the Ascendant Shadow. He also does not realize that the Ascendant Shadow is using him by condensing all power into a centralized point of failure in Aurengate. He is a Putin-like figure, has had his heirs killed.
* Once in power, Izuriel begins to invade neighboring kingdoms and consolidate them until the entire continent is essentially under his banner. He does this over 1,000 years by perpetuating a false lineage of heirs, each of whom is really him in another aspect, given his power to shapeshift from the power given to him by the Ascendant Shadow
This timeline not only frames your world’s history within cycles of rise, fall, and rebirth, influenced by powerful magical artifacts and dark entities but also sets a backdrop for the players’ adventures against a rich historical canvas. They will uncover lost truths, face consequences of ancient conflicts, and perhaps alter the cycle of violence that has plagued their world for millennia.


Proposed alternate Ancient History of Valisar
The Era of Beginnings (~10,000–8,000 years ago)
The discovery or creation of the Eldspyre, a cosmic artifact of unimaginable power, marked the dawn of civilization in Valisar. The Eldspyre served as a source of magic and balance, channeling the forces of light (the Spark) and darkness (the Shadow). Under its influence, the sorcerer-kings rose to power, guiding the continent into an era of harmony and prosperity.
The Eldspyre Custodians were formed to safeguard the artifact and maintain its delicate balance. With their guidance, the Aetherforged Machina—ancient devices designed to stabilize leylines and amplify magic—were constructed, linking the lands in unity. Spirits, as natural guardians of the world, worked alongside mortals to sustain this equilibrium.
The Era of Expansion (~8,000–6,000 years ago)
This golden age saw the flourishing of the Eldspyre’s influence. The Custodians established leyline nodes across Valisar, embedding the Aetherforged Machina in temples and cities. The sorcerer-kings, wielding unparalleled magical power, ruled wisely but grew increasingly ambitious.
Philosophical divisions began to form among the Custodians and their allies. Some viewed the Eldspyre as a tool to create and sustain life, while others believed its power was too dangerous to remain unchecked. These tensions set the stage for later conflict.
The Era of the Great Sundering (~6,000–5,000 years ago)
A cataclysm shattered the Eldspyre, unleashing chaos across Valisar. Some records attribute this disaster to internal conflicts among the Custodians, while others suggest an external force exploited their divisions. This event fractured the continent and gave birth to the Ascendant Shadow, a malevolent force formed from the Eldspyre’s darker aspects.
The Great Sundering marked the decline of the sorcerer-kings. Leylines faltered, and spirits weakened as corruption spread. The surviving Custodians tried to restore order, but their influence waned as Valisar descended into chaos.
The Era of Dark Ages (~5,000–3,000 years ago)
Following the Sundering, Valisar entered an age of decline. Kingdoms rose and fell, each vying for control over fragments of the Eldspyre’s legacy. The Aetherforged Machina became relics of a forgotten age, misunderstood and misused by those who uncovered them.
Spirits retreated as corruption spread, severing the bonds between mortals and the natural world. The Ascendant Shadow grew stronger in the absence of balance, its influence felt through whispered omens and strange, dark forces.
The Era of Rebirth and Enlightenment (~3,000–1,000 years ago)
A flawed renaissance emerged as fragments of the Eldspyre’s teachings were rediscovered. Scholars and rulers sought to harness its remnants, but their limited understanding often caused more harm than good. This period also saw the rise of new ideologies, including a growing mistrust of spirits. Some began to see them as unpredictable and dangerous, a sentiment manipulated by those seeking power.
It was during this era that Izuriel Sakazarac first emerged—or so the records claim. In truth, Izuriel was not born into the Sakazarac line. Instead, he was a cunning sorcerer from a much less noble house who struck a pact with the Ascendant Shadow, also becoming a warlock in the process, granting him longevity and immense magical power at the cost of his soul.
Izuriel orchestrated his rise by infiltrating the Sakazarac lineage during a period of instability. He killed the ruling family and assumed the identity of the “rightful heir,” declaring himself Izuriel I. Through propaganda, sorcery, and assassination, he solidified his power, presenting himself as a divine ruler chosen to guide Valisar into a new age.
The Era of the Sakazarac Empire (~1,000 years ago–Present)
Izuriel has ruled Aurengate for over 1,000 years, but not as a single figure. To maintain his grip on power, he has carefully crafted a false lineage of rulers, masquerading as the “heir” of each successive generation of the Sakazarac dynasty. When the people believe their king has died, Izuriel simply adopts a new identity as his “son” or “descendant,” perpetuating the illusion of a continuous line of benevolent rulers.
Under his rule, Aurengate has become a sprawling metropolis of wealth and power. Its people worship the Sun-King as a divine protector, unaware that their prosperity is built on lies and exploitation. Izuriel’s propaganda machine blames the spirits for every disaster, positioning himself as the only force capable of protecting humanity.
Behind the scenes, Izuriel’s true goals are far darker. Through his pact with the Ascendant Shadow, he has corrupted leylines and desecrated temples, systematically weakening the spirits to pave the way for the Shadow’s full manifestation. The upcoming Bone Moon—a celestial event prophesied to herald great change—marks the culmination of his plans. With the Shadow’s power fully unleashed, Izuriel intends to reshape Valisar into an eternal empire under his rule.
Izuriel’s Legacy and Propaganda
        1.        The False Lineage:
Izuriel has fabricated a dynasty, claiming to be the son or grandson of his past identities. This strategy isolates him from the people, making him an enigmatic, almost godlike figure while masking his true age and identity.
        2.        Blame the Spirits:
Izuriel’s propaganda paints spirits as dangerous and chaotic, responsible for Valisar’s calamities. This narrative justifies his war against them and further alienates mortals from their natural guardians.
        3.        The Sun-King’s Image:
Through symbols, rituals, and public displays of power, Izuriel presents himself as a savior. His mastery of solar magic allows him to stage divine interventions that awe and subdue the population.


The Sakazarac Dynasty: Names and Personas
Each “king” is part of the fabricated Sakazarac lineage, complete with their own invented history, achievements, and downfall. Izuriel carefully orchestrates each transition to maintain his aura of divine right and continuity.
1. Izuriel I, The Radiant Flame (1,000–850 Years Ago)
        •        Persona: The first of the Sakazarac “dynasty,” Izuriel I was presented as a noble savior who unified the fragmented regions surrounding Aurengate under his divine light.
        •        Signature Style: Grandiose displays of solar magic, consolidating power through fear and awe.
        •        Key Propaganda: Izuriel claimed to be “chosen by the gods” to reignite civilization after the dark ages.
        •        Fate: Officially “died in his sleep” after decades of rule, leaving his “kingdom” in the hands of his son.
2. Calyric I, The Builder King (850–700 Years Ago)
        •        Persona: The supposed son of Izuriel I, Calyric I was characterized as a visionary builder who expanded Aurengate’s infrastructure, including its canals and celestial observatories.
        •        Signature Style: Focused on architectural achievements and reinforcing the dynasty’s “divine right” through public works.
        •        Key Propaganda: Positioned himself as a scholar-king who honored the spirits but proclaimed their diminishing relevance.
        •        Fate: “Died in battle” defending Aurengate against a fabricated threat, becoming a martyr.
3. Tyserion I, The Golden Blade (700–550 Years Ago)
        •        Persona: Calyric’s “son” and a warrior-king who forged the Aurengate military into an unstoppable force.
        •        Signature Style: Martial prowess combined with overwhelming solar magic, positioning himself as the protector of humanity. Forced the Wizards of the Citidaea into submission under the Spirit of Pride. Expanded the bounds of the Sakazarac empire considerably to include The Sweeping Plains and the Fuhran Dessert.
        •        Key Propaganda: Launched campaigns against “spirit loyalists,” accusing them of treachery and chaos.
        •        Fate: Claimed to have been “struck down by shadow magic” while leading his forces, though his military victories were celebrated posthumously.
4. Alorys I, The Dawn Shepherd (550–400 Years Ago)
        •        Persona: The “peaceful” heir of Tyserion I, Alorys was framed as a ruler of compassion who brought a golden age of diplomacy and prosperity.
        •        Signature Style: Patronized the arts, sponsoring bardic guilds to spread songs glorifying the Sakazarac lineage.
        •        Key Propaganda: Used diplomacy to “prove” the Sakazaracs’ divine connection to light and peace.
        •        Fate: “Retired to meditation,” ascending to the heavens as a saint. His “son” took his place soon after.
5. Delyric I, The Iron Sun (400–250 Years Ago)
        •        Persona: Delyric was the “pragmatic” ruler, focusing on securing Aurengate’s borders and cementing alliances with nearby kingdoms.
        •        Signature Style: Harsh policies that centralized power in the monarchy and removed dissenters with calculated precision.
        •        Key Propaganda: Used fear of external threats and the spirits’ “growing chaos” to justify his authoritarian measures.
        •        Fate: Assassinated by a “traitorous noble,” inspiring his heir to “reclaim the throne.”
6. Ilzaren I, The Resplendent King (250–100 Years Ago)
        •        Persona: The charismatic and theatrical ruler, Ilzaren embraced the identity of a divine figurehead, holding elaborate festivals and ceremonies to celebrate the Sakazarac lineage.
        •        Signature Style: Relied heavily on religious imagery, reinforcing the monarchy’s supposed connection to celestial forces.
        •        Key Propaganda: Introduced the Solar Creed, a state religion glorifying the Sun-King and condemning spirits as heretical.
        •        Fate: Claimed to have ascended into a celestial form, leaving his mortal body behind.
7. Izuriel II, The Eternal Light (100 Years Ago–Present)
        •        Persona: Returning to his original name, Izuriel II presents himself as the culmination of the Sakazarac dynasty, a ruler destined to bring about the “final dawn.”
        •        Signature Style: Combines elements of all his previous personas, amplifying his divine image as both a warlord and a spiritual savior.
        •        Key Propaganda: Uses the Bone Moon Prophecy to paint himself as humanity’s last hope against spiritual chaos.
        •        Current Strategy: Izuriel II rarely appears in public, maintaining an air of mystery and divine distance while his lieutenants govern Aurengate.
Traits Across All Personas
        1.        Symbolism and Continuity:
        •        Each ruler adopts variations of the Sun-Symbol as their sigil, with subtle changes to match their reign’s focus (e.g., a golden sword for Tyserion, a radiant crown for Ilzaren).
        •        Public art and statues depicting each ruler deliberately maintain a resemblance to Izuriel’s original form, suggesting divine lineage.
        2.        Manipulated History:
        •        Izuriel frequently rewrites history, erasing inconsistencies and ensuring his personas are viewed as heroic paragons. Disloyal scholars or records are systematically eliminated.
        3.        Isolation from the Public:
        •        Izuriel’s “divine distance” has grown over the generations, making him seem more godlike and less mortal with each iteration. His “heirs” are always announced with elaborate rituals to reinforce this mystique.


The Grand Deception of Izuriel (Added 8/19/25)
Phase I – The Radiant Flame (1,000–850 years ago)
 Izuriel begins by creating the illusion of a noble savior (Izuriel I). He unifies the splintered states under Aurengate, not simply to secure power, but to set the foundation for a state religion centered on the Eldspyre as Light. This was the first sleight of hand: a subtle rebranding of the Eldspyre from a chaotic, primal source into an imperial-sanctioned font of order.
* Chess piece placed: The idea that only the Sakazarac dynasty can safeguard magic.

* Hidden goal: Ensure future generations reject spirits as rivals and grow dependent on imperial ideology.

Phase II – The Builder & The Blade (850–550 years ago)
 Through his alter egos (Calyric the Builder, Tyserion the Golden Blade), Izuriel locks down infrastructure and the military. Roads, fortresses, and skyship docks aren’t merely civic projects—they are leyline anchors, built over sites of spiritual resonance, binding the land’s lifeblood into imperial stone.
   * Chess piece placed: Control of the continent’s leylines disguised as progress.

   * Hidden goal: Slowly siphon spiritual vitality into Aurengate, starving rival powers.

Phase III – The Shepherd & The Iron Sun (550–250 years ago)
 Through Alorys the Dawn Shepherd, he paints the dynasty as a benevolent line of peace-bringers. Then, as Delyric the Iron Sun, he pivots to fear—external threats, heretics, spirits—always positioning the empire as sole protector. These dual faces (kindness and tyranny) trained the people to accept contradictory truths, priming them for future propaganda.
      * Chess piece placed: Normalization of cognitive dissonance—light as both salvation and terror.

      * Hidden goal: Condition the people to accept any imperial decree without question.

Phase IV – The Resplendent King & The Creed (250–100 years ago)
 As Ilzaren the Resplendent, Izuriel introduces the Solar Creed. This religion cements his dynasty not merely as rulers, but as avatars of divine light. It demonizes spirits, rewriting myths so that the Ascendant Shadow is framed not as an existential corruption, but as something only the Sun-King can banish.

Lore of Magic Users in Valisar
In the world of Valisar, magic is a revered and mysterious force, emanating from the cosmic heart of creation itself, known as the Eldspyre. Though not common among the general populace, magic is an integral part of the world, shaping its history, culture, and conflicts. Here's an overview of the magic users, the source of their powers, and their relationship with the Eldspyre:
1. Origins of Magic: The Eldspyre
                     * The Eldspyre: This is the primal forge from which all magic in Valisar flows. It is both a concept and a reality, a flame that burns at the core of existence, giving life to all magical energies.
                     * Tapping into The Eldspyre: Magic users learn to tap into this ultimate source, channeling its energy through their particular practices and talents. However, the Eldspyre is not merely a tool; it's a living, chaotic force with its will and whims.
2. Types of Magic Users and Their Sources
                     * Wizards: Wizards study magic through rigorous academic means. They learn ancient texts, runes, and formulas to draw directly from the Eldspyre. Many are scholars and researchers, seeking to unravel the secrets of the Eldspyre itself.
                     * Sorcerers: Born with innate magic, Sorcerers have a natural connection to the Eldspyre. Their powers are often wild and unpredictable, and some believe they are chosen or cursed by the Eldspyre for reasons unknown.
                     * Druids: Druids draw magic from nature, believing that the Eldspyre's flame lives within the earth, trees, and waters. They view the Eldspyre as a part of the natural cycle of life and death.
                     * Clerics and Paladins: These divine magic users believe their powers come from deities or celestial beings that have mastered or are connected to the Eldspyre. They may see the Eldspyre as a holy flame, a divine test, or a celestial challenge.
                     * Warlocks: Warlocks gain their magic through pacts with powerful entities, some of which may be connected to or even born from the Eldspyre. These pacts often involve complex relationships and deep mysteries surrounding the Eldspyre.
                     * Bards: Through music and art, Bards channel the creative spark of the Eldspyre, translating its chaotic energy into performance, charm, and enchantment.
3. Tensions and Conflicts
                     * Fear and Reverence: Magic, being uncommon and tied to the enigmatic Eldspyre, can be both revered and feared. While some see magic users as sages, healers, or protectors, others view them with suspicion, attributing disasters or curses to their meddling with the Eldspyre.
                     * Manipulation vs. Respect: Some believe that the Eldspyre should not be tampered with, while others seek to master and manipulate it. This creates tension among magic users and even entire magical traditions, leading to conflicts, debates, and even wars.
                     * The Mystery of the Eldspyre: The ultimate source of all magic is not fully understood, and many seek to uncover its secrets. This quest can lead to obsession, madness, or enlightenment, and it can also draw the attention of ancient beings, guardians, or enemies connected to the Eldspyre.
Conclusion
The magic users of Valisar draw their powers from the enigmatic Eldspyre, each in their unique way, reflecting different philosophies, practices, and cultural beliefs. This connection to the Eldspyre creates a complex tapestry of awe, conflict, and mystery surrounding magic in Valisar, providing a rich background for storytelling, character development, and world-building. Whether a wise village sage, a cunning urban wizard, or a mysterious sorcerer with a mystical lineage, magic users in Valisar navigate a world filled with wonder and danger, where the very source of their power may hold secrets that can change the fate of the entire world.

Colloquial terms for machina


                        * Starforged - A name that might be given by a society that believes the technology was crafted by celestial beings or influenced by the stars.
                        * Ancestrine - A word that would resonate with a society that believes the ancient technology is the work of their ancestors.
                        * Eldertech - Used by a society that acknowledges the age and ancient origin of the technology.
                        * Duskmade - A term from a society that believes the tech comes from a 'dusk' or twilight era; a time of both enlightenment and shadow.
                        * Skygift - A moniker given by those who believe that the technology was a gift or boon from the heavens.
                        * Lostech - A somewhat somber term from civilizations that feel the knowledge to create such technology has been lost to time.
                        * Godforge - A name from those who firmly believe only gods could have made such intricate and powerful machines.
                        * Mystmach - Used by a society that sees the technology as both mystical and mechanical in nature.
                        * Primetech - A term from those who think the tech comes from the 'prime' era or the golden age of their world.
                        * Oldsparks - A playful or even slightly derogatory term for ancient tech, suggesting it's a bit rickety or unreliable.
                        * Nightsilvered - Maybe the ancient tech has a silvery sheen or the knowledge to make them was believed to be harvested during moonlit nights.
                        * Galeroot - If the tech seems integrated with nature or made of organic components, suggesting it's as old as trees or foundational elements of the world.
                        * Echomach - Named for technology that seems to echo or replicate effects or power from an older era.
                        * Lorestone - Used if the tech is primarily in the form of crystalline or stone-like constructs containing vast knowledge.
                        * Ancienttech - A straightforward, descriptive term used by those who acknowledge the ancient origins of discovered machines.
                        * Relicwork/Reliccraft - Evokes a sense of lost history and craftsmanship. Common among scholars.
                        * Godsforge - Used by those who believe the machines to be crafted by gods rather than mortals.
                        * Dawnforged - A reference to a mythical "dawn era" when such advanced technology supposedly originated.
                        * Starcraft/Stargift - Indicates a belief that the technology came from celestial beings who bestowed it to certain ancient peoples.
                        * Ancestorcraft - Suggests the technology belonged to honored ancestors rather than the gods.
                        * Elderwork - Respectfully acknowledges the great age and mystery of the machines.
                        * Relicsteel - Referring directly to the materials used, many of which are rare or long-forgotten alloys.
                        * Ruinwork - Disparaging term used by those who believe tampering with such things leads to disaster or ruin.
                        * Voidcraft - Sinister term used by those who believe the technology came from dark powers or void entities.
                        * Chaoswork - Indicates a belief that the technology is too reckless, dangerous and chaotic to be used safely.

But this connection, as powerful as it may be, risks creating an imbalance in the fabric of our universe—an imbalance you yourselves might have to restore."
The Talisman
Understanding the weight of Elandra's words and what might lie ahead, Lirielle's parents began to prepare for the day they might need to restore the cosmic balance. As part of their preparations, they created a talisman imbued with their love, wisdom, and protective energies. As Lirielle grew older, they fastened it around her neck, a physical embodiment of their eternal love and guidance that she would always carry with her.
The Celestial Gate
When the day of reckoning arrived, a celestial gate materialized, radiant and full of swirling stardust, confirming Elandra's prophecy. Lirielle's parents knew what they had to do. They took their daughter's hands and spoke words of unconditional love and timeless wisdom. "We'll be walking through this gate with you, but only you will walk back out. Remember, we are always with you—in every star that lights your path, in every whisper of the wind through the trees."
The Walk of Destiny
Hand in hand, the family walked through the celestial gate. There was a moment of ethereal stillness, a meeting of mortal and immortal, finite and infinite. Then Lirielle felt her parents' hands slowly slip away from her own. When she returned through the celestial gate, she was alone, but for the appearance of Lumina, the celestial fox who would become her guide and guardian.
A Soul-Deep Understanding
Upon her return, Elandra helped Lirielle understand the magnitude of her parents' sacrifice. Though young, Lirielle grasped the essential truth: her parents had restored the cosmic balance, making her own life's journey possible. They had become a part of the very cosmos that she was now destined to explore and protect, forever interwoven with her own tapestry of fate.

Luigi Bonemoon
Level 3 Human “The Genie” Warlock
Luigi Bonemoon, once a vibrant and adventurous relic hunter, is now a 44-year-old human who lives a life of seclusion, haunted by a dark prophecy that he uncovered 15 years ago. In his prime, Luigi was known for his gregarious and jovial nature. He had an insatiable curiosity about the past and dedicated his life to uncovering the secrets of the ancient civilizations of Valisar, the world in which he lived. His adventurous spirit led him on numerous expeditions, digging through ancient ruins and unearthing long-forgotten artifacts. However, everything changed during an expedition in a particularly remote and mysterious site. There, deep beneath the earth, Luigi stumbled upon a hidden chamber containing a relic vessel connected to the Eldspyre but he didn’t know it. As he examined the vessel, he accidentally unleashed a powerful Spirit, an inscrutible being made of gears and rings and angel wings and an enormous unblinking eye. For mechanical game purposes this is an Efreeti, but we are flavoring this as a Spirit in the world of Valisar.. The Spirit, grateful for its release and intrigued by Luigi's knowledge of the ancient civilizations, offered him a pact: power and knowledge in exchange for his service. In a moment of weakness, driven by his desire to learn more about the ancient civilizations and prevent the prophecy from coming true, Luigi accepted the pact. Unbeknownst to him, this decision would forever alter the course of his life. The prophecy that Luigi uncovered spoke of the Bonemoon, a celestial event that would bring about the annihilation of all life and lead to the ruin of Valisar, much like the ancient civilizations that once thrived there. The prophecy consumed Luigi, filling him with a sense of dread and hopelessness. Fearing that his actions as a relic hunter might inadvertently hasten the prophecy's fulfillment, Luigi swore off relic hunting and retreated from society. He now lives alone in a small cabin a few miles away from a nearby town, rarely venturing out except to buy supplies. Although he still harbors a deep love for the ancient civilizations of Valisar, he is haunted by the fear that his actions may advance the world toward ruin. The once adventurous and spirited man is now a shadow of his former self, weighed down by the burden of the prophecy and the pact he made with the Efreeti. As the time of the Bonemoon approaches, Luigi is torn between his desire to prevent the prophecy from coming true and the obligations of his pact with the Efreeti. Will he find a way to avert disaster, or will he succumb to the dark forces that threaten to consume the world?
Luigi does not know he signed a pact with the spirit 15 years ago, and hasn't seen the genie in any recognizable form since


But now, 15 years later, the genie has appeared to him in again as the clock angel form and come to collect the service that Luigi swore in his pact unwittingly. The spirit has also issued Luigi a familiar, a small fuzzy blob that functions like Tattle and Tale in Majora’s Mask, does not always tell the truth to Luigi, is inscrutable, and is mostly a spy for the spirit.
