Session Five (1/29/23)






Amidst the shadow-draped wilderness of Ebon Forest, the Temple of Nitriti stands as a solemn relic of a bygone era. Shrouded in an air of forgotten majesty, the temple exudes an aura of ancient reverence, now tainted by the touch of time and neglect. Its once-proud walls, crafted from somber-hued stone, rise starkly against the dense foliage that encroaches upon the clearing.


The temple's facade, carved with intricate motifs symbolizing the night and its celestial mysteries, is now partially obscured by creeping ivy and moss. These natural elements seem to be in a silent battle with the structure, reclaiming the temple back to the earth from which it was hewn. The grand entrance, framed by towering columns, stands as a gaping maw leading into the dim recesses of the sacred halls within. The doorway, bereft of its original doors, appears as an open portal to a realm of forgotten deities and faded rituals.


Above the entrance, a fractured bas-relief depicts the visage of Nitriti, the spirit of Night, her features worn yet still exuding a sense of ethereal grace and power. The air here is heavy with a sense of expectancy, as if the temple itself is holding its breath, waiting for the return of its long-lost patrons.


The surrounding grounds of the temple, once meticulously maintained, are now overrun with wild undergrowth, and the path leading to the temple is faint, more a memory of passage than a clear way. Scattered around the structure are remnants of old offerings and rituals, now overgrown and barely recognizable under the weight of years.


As the party approaches, the wind whispers through the trees, carrying with it the faintest hint of ancient incense and a whisper of the mysteries that lie within the temple's hallowed, yet desecrated, halls.


Upon entering the temple, the party is immediately enveloped in a dense, oppressive atmosphere. The entry chamber, once a place of silent reverence, now bears the scars of desecration. The walls, which once depicted elegant celestial murals dedicated to Nitriti, are now defaced with crude symbols and drawings. These symbols are rough, almost childlike in execution, but carry a sinister energy – swirls, jagged lines, and distorted figures in the throes of suffering.


The hall is lined with statues of Nitriti, each defaced to various degrees. Some have been toppled over, others have goblin-made masks or totems crudely attached.




The main antechamber has a statue puzzle that must be solved by first understanding the Ascendant Shadow legend. This statue is missing the crown, the cape, and the scepter. Behind the statue are two enormous circular stone doors that overlap across a massive archway.





In investigating the available temple rooms, the party discovers the etchings and walls of the temple have been covered with scrawlings and crude drawings. In the other rooms of the temple, each party member experiences a flashback before they receive one of the key items for the statue.

Ancient Celestial Observatory: 
The observatory is a circular chamber, its walls lined with polished obsidian that reflects the night sky as if the room itself breaches the boundary between the material and the celestial. At the center stands a grand orrery, an intricate mechanical model of Valisar's cosmos, with planets, moons, and stars represented by precious gems and metals, orbiting around a glowing representation of the Eldspyre. This orrery is not only a work of art but a powerful instrument of observation and prophecy, animated by the unseen forces of Eldertech and the will of Nitriti.


The ceiling of the observatory is a marvel in itself—an arching dome of transparent crystal that frames the heavens in its entirety. The dark glass dome always shows the night sky, regardless of what time it actually is.


Around the perimeter of the room stand several telescopes of varying sizes and designs, each an amalgamation of Eldertech and traditional craftsmanship. Ancient Machina Codex:
Contents of the Ancient Machina Codex
What It Contains: 
It specifically does not call things “Eldertech” but instead “Aetherforged”, a term the PCs will need to put together as belonging to another culture outside of Ebonvale
                           * Aetherforged Principles: Theoretical foundations and practical applications of Aetherforged, including energy harnessing, transference, and storage.
                           * Machinery Schematics: Detailed blueprints of various Aetherforged devices, from simple tools to complex constructs like the celestial observatory and gear doors.
                           * Runic Programming: Instructions on how to use ancient runes to program and control Aetherforged devices, blending magic with technology.
                           * Astronavigation: Lore on celestial navigation using Aetherforged devices, crucial for interpreting star maps and navigating ley lines.
                           * Historical Accounts: Chronicles of the ancient civilization that created the Aetherforged, offering insights into their society, culture, and downfall.
What It Does Not Contain:
                           * Specific Locations: While the Codex discusses principles and applications, it deliberately omits the locations of significant Aetherforged sites to protect them from exploitation or desecration.
                           * Modern Applications: The Codex does not bridge the gap between ancient Aetherforged and contemporary magic or technology directly, leaving room for discovery and innovation by the PCs.
                           * Complete Aetherforged Lexicon: The language of the ancient creators is partially translated, leaving some of the text mysterious and open to interpretation.
                           * Detailed Creation Methods: While the Codex explains how Aetherforged devices work, it often lacks the step-by-step instructions for creating these devices from scratch, requiring PCs to experiment and learn through trial and error.
                           * The Full Scope of Aetherforged Power: The Codex hints at the vast potential of Aetherforged but stops short of revealing its most powerful applications, suggesting that some knowledge was too dangerous to record or has been lost to time.
This Ancient Machina Codex serves as a rich source of lore and potential quests for the PCs, providing them with knowledge to explore while also emphasizing the mysteries and challenges that lie in fully understanding and mastering Aetherforged.
